#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../../WikiData/WD~Songhua_River,210326]] 

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Songhua or Sunghwa River (also Haixi or Xingal, Russian: Сунгари, romanized: Sungari) 
> is one of the primary rivers of China, and the longest tributary of the Amur. 
> 
> It flows about 1,897 km (1,179 mi) from Changbai Mountains on the China–North Korea border 
> through China's northeastern Jilin and Heilongjiang provinces.
>
> The river drains 557,180 km2 (215,130 sq mi) of land, and has an annual discharge of 76.2 km3/a (2,410 m3/s) to 81.77 km3/a (2,591 m3/s).
>
> The extreme flatness of the Northeast China Plain has caused the river to meander over time, 
> filling the wide plain with oxbow lakes, as remnants of the previous paths of the river.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Songhua%20River)
